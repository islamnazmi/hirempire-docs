## Understanding Plan Limits

### Active vs Total Jobs

<Info>
  **Important Distinction**: You can create unlimited total jobs on all plans, but only a certain number can be "active" (accepting applications) at once based on your plan.
</Info>

**Example Workflow**:
1. Create 20 different job postings (unlimited)
2. Activate only 3-30 jobs based on your plan
3. Deactivate filled positions
4. Activate new jobs as needed

### Company Management

**Multi-Company Benefits**:
- Separate branding for each company
- Individual job tracking per company
- Different application processes
- Isolated candidate pools (optional)

**Agency Use Cases**:
- Manage multiple client companies

- Track performance by client
- Streamlined multi-client workflow

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change plans anytime?">
    **Yes, absolutely!** You can upgrade or downgrade your plan at any time. 
    
    - **Upgrades**: Take effect immediately with prorated billing
    - **Downgrades**: Take effect at next billing cycle
    - **No penalties**: No cancellation fees or long-term commitments
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What happens if I exceed my active job limit?">
    **No Worries!** Your existing jobs continue to work normally.
    
    - You simply cannot activate new jobs until you're within your limit
    - Deactivate filled positions to free up slots
    - Consider upgrading for more active job capacity
    - All inactive jobs remain accessible
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Do I lose my data if I downgrade?">
    **Never!** All your data is preserved regardless of plan changes.
    
    - All candidates, jobs, and companies remain accessible
    - Historical data stays intact
    - You can always upgrade again to regain access to premium features
    - Export options available for data portability
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Is there a free trial for paid features?">
    **Free Forever Plan**: Try most features risk-free with our generous free plan
    
    **AI Features**: Currently no separate trial, but the Starter plan is affordable for testing
    
    **Money-Back Policy**: Contact support if you're not satisfied with paid features
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
