Sourcing lets you describe the candidate you want in plain English and get back a ranked list of real people with verified contact info, pulled from LinkedIn and other public professional sources. No boolean strings, no scraping, no spreadsheets.

<Check>
  Each search consumes Sourcing credits from your workspace plan.
</Check>

## How sourcing works

  <Card title="1. Describe" icon="message">

## Running a search

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Sourcing page">
    Go to [**Sourcing**](https://app.hirempire.com/dashboard/sourcing) from your dashboard sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe who you're looking for">
    Use the prompt box and write a natural description. Be specific about the things that matter to you.

    Example prompts:
    - `Senior React developer with fintech experience in London`
    - `Product designer who has shipped B2B SaaS, based in Berlin or remote EU`
    - `Sales engineer with API and developer-tools background in the US`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Press Enter or click Search">
    The search typically takes 30 to 90 seconds while Hirempire scans profiles, pulls contact data, and generates AI summaries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review results">
    Candidates appear as cards with name, headline, location, LinkedIn and GitHub links, email, phone, and a short AI fit summary.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Press `Enter` to search. Use `Shift + Enter` to add a new line inside your prompt.
</Tip>

## Understanding the results

Each candidate card includes:

| Field | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Photo** | Profile picture from LinkedIn or GitHub |
| **Name and headline** | Current role and short pitch |
| **Location** | City or region |
| **LinkedIn** | Direct link to the LinkedIn profile |
| **GitHub** | Direct link when the candidate has a public GitHub |
| **Email** | Verified work or personal email when available |
| **Phone** | Phone number when available |
| **AI summary** | One-line fit signal generated for your prompt |

<Info>
  Contact details (email, phone) are best-effort. Some profiles return a LinkedIn link only, especially for senior or privacy-conscious candidates.
</Info>

## Selecting and acting on candidates

- Click the checkbox on any card to select it. A counter appears at the top right showing how many candidates you have selected.
- Click the email, LinkedIn, GitHub, or phone icons on a card to open or copy that contact channel directly.
- Click **Clear** to deselect everything and start again.

## Search history

The left sidebar lists your workspace's previous searches with the original prompt and how long ago it ran.

- Click any past search to reopen its results without spending credits again.
- Click **New Search** at the top of the sidebar to start a fresh prompt.
- Collapse the sidebar with the arrow icon to give yourself more room on wider result grids.

<Tip>
  Past searches are shared at the workspace level, so teammates can see and reuse each other's sourcing work.
</Tip>

## Connecting LinkedIn (optional)

Sourcing itself runs against Hirempire's own data and does not require a LinkedIn login. You can still connect a LinkedIn account in **Settings > LinkedIn** if you want to:

- Post jobs to LinkedIn from inside Hirempire
- Send connection requests or InMail from connected accounts in future workflows

<Warning>
  Connecting LinkedIn is only available on paid plans, not on Free or during a free trial. Each user connects their own LinkedIn account.
</Warning>

To connect:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open settings">
    Go to [**Settings > LinkedIn**](https://app.hirempire.com/dashboard/settings/linkedin).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect LinkedIn">
    Accept the consent panel to authorize Hirempire to act on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a posting company">
    Once connected, select the LinkedIn Company Page you want to post jobs as.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Writing better prompts

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific about must-haves">
    Vague prompts like `developer in Europe` produce vague results. Try `Senior Go developer with payments or fintech experience, based in Western Europe` instead.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Mention seniority and scope">
    Use words like `junior`, `senior`, `staff`, `lead`, `head of`, or specific years of experience.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Include industry or product context">
    Context like `B2B SaaS`, `marketplaces`, `consumer mobile`, or `regulated healthcare` sharpens the matches considerably.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Be honest about location">
    Say `remote OK`, `must be in CET timezone`, or list specific cities. The AI uses location as a hard filter.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips

- Use one prompt per role. Avoid trying to source two different jobs in one search.
- If results feel off, refine the prompt and run a second search rather than scrolling endlessly.
- Save high-signal candidates to your [Candidates Bank](/candidates/candidates-bank) so you can search and reuse them later.
- For passive sourcing while browsing LinkedIn yourself, use the [Chrome Extension](/candidates/chrome) instead.
